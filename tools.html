Tools

The Basics

A phone;
Your own unique way of seeing the world coupled with a little bit of knowledge;
Lots of sunlight/available light;

A set of mini lenses(made for phones), if you want to get serious;
A tripod(with an adapter), or mini tripod. Especially needed if you plan on shooting long exposure and/or HDR;
A flashlight—or any other portable light fixture—is quite fun for experimenting in dark places;

If you're really serious about it, it might be a good idea to try calibrating the colors of your phone screen. There are devices and apps to help you with that but since I have not done this on my phone, I won't recommend any specific products. Searching for "screen color calibration" should give you a starting point(look for the smaller and more portable devices because, not only they'll be more than enough, but also most color calibration systems are quite expensive).


Apps

Camera:

  • Built-In Camera App — Can get you started, but you'll really need manual controls to get the results you envision, so this one will very quickly become a limitation;
  • Lightroom CC — Manual controls, and RAW.
  • Manual Camera (Android-only) — Manual controls, and RAW.
  • Manual — RAW Camera (iOS-only) — Manual controls, and RAW.
  • Hypocam — Dedicated to Black & White. Several features, including colored filters.

Editing:

  • Built-In Photo Editor — In most cases, I don't do any post-processing and try to get most of the shot in-camera, but when I do want to make adjustments, it's usually a slight Crop, Contrast or Saturation adjustment, so the built-in app is more than enough.
  • Snapseed — Lots of tools and great controls. It has double exposure, selective adjustments, curves, film grain and many more.
  • Lightroom CC — The presets system is quite useful for small adjustments.
  • Pixlr — Many tools and lots of presets. Some presets are pretty nice. It also has a nice collage tool.
  • Hypocam — Dedicated to Black & White. Several features, including colored filters and film grain.
  • LightX — The controls aren't great but I like the splash(selective adjustment) tool.

Scouting:

  • Sun Locator (Android-only) — Shows you at what position the Sun will be throughout the day, and other info.
  • Sun Scout (iOS-only) — Shows you at what position the Sun will be throughout the day, and other info.

I'd recommend you spend some time with each one of them to see which ones you prefer.
